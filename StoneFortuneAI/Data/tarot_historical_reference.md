## PART 1: MAJOR ARCANA (0–21)

### Structural Keys
- **22 cards** correspond to the 22 Hebrew letters (Aleph–Tau)
- Organized as **three Septenaries** (groups of 7) + a Ternary of Transition (Papus)
- 1st Septenary (1–7): **Theogony / God** — corresponds to Yod
- 2nd Septenary (8–13): **Androgony / Man** — corresponds to He (1st)
- 3rd Septenary (14–19): **Cosmogony / Nature** — corresponds to Vau
- Ternary of Transition (19–21 + 0): connects Major and Minor Arcana — corresponds to 2nd He
- Mathers' memorable sentence: *"The Human Will (1) enlightened by Science (2) and manifested by Action (3) should find its Realisation (4) in deeds of Mercy and Beneficence (5). The Wise Disposition (6) gives Victory (7) through Equilibrium (8) and Prudence (9) over Fortune (10). Fortitude (11), sanctified by Sacrifice of Self (12), will triumph over Death (13), and a Wise Combination (14) enables him to defy Fate (15). In each Misfortune (16) he will see the Star of Hope (17) shine through the twilight of Deception (18); and ultimate Happiness (19) will be the Result (20). Folly (0) brings about an evil Reward (21)."*

### 0. THE FOOL
**Hebrew Letter:** Shin (ש) — one of the 3 Mother Letters; sign of relative duration and vibrating movement
**Astronomy/Element:** Air (Shin = Mother letter of Air)
**Papus Number:** 21st card (unnumbered)

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A man with a fool's cap, dressed like a jester, stick and bundle over shoulder. A butterfly of pleasure lures him on; a tiger or dog attacks him from behind. Signifies **Folly, Expiation.**
- **Waite:** Depicts youth at precipice edge, gazing upward, white rose in hand. Dog at his heels. Sun shines behind him. The bag on the wand holds four elements. The figure is on the verge of stepping into an abyss — pure potential, the divine madness.
- **Ouspensky:** "Man. An ordinary man. A separate man. The uninitiate. Lower consciousness. The end of a ray not knowing its relation to the centre."
- **Papus:** A careless-looking man wearing a fool's cap, torn clothes, bundle upon shoulder, walking quietly while a dog (or crocodile in some versions) threatens him. Symbol of the Flesh and its gratification.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Folly, mania, extravagance, intoxication, delirium, frenzy, bewrayment.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Negligence, absence, distribution, carelessness, apathy, nullity, vanity.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Folly, Expiation, Wavering.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Hesitation, Instability, Trouble arising therefrom.

### I. THE MAGICIAN (The Juggler)
**Hebrew Letter:** Beth (ב) — House; Mercury; Intelligence of Transparency; joins Kether to Binah
**Astronomy:** Mercury
**Foster Case Attribution:** Beth = Mercury; "Life and Death: Specialization implies construction and destruction"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** Before a table covered with appliances stands a juggler, one hand upraised holding a wand, the other pointing downward. Body and arms form the shape of Hebrew letter Aleph. Symbolizes **Will.**
- **Waite:** A young man in the garden of white and red roses. Above his head the lemniscate (∞). On the table before him: cup, wand, sword, pentacle — the four suits. Right hand raises wand to heaven; left points to earth. "As above, so below." White wand = pure will. Red roses = desire purified.
- **Ouspensky:** "Man. Superman. The Initiate. The Occultist. Higher consciousness. Human Logos. The Kabbalistic Adam Kadmon. Humanity. Homo Sapiens."
- **Papus (Card 1):** The Absolute Being. Active, positive principle. God the Father, Osiris, Brahma, Jupiter. Creator/Divine. Yod of the Tetragrammaton.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Skill, diplomacy, address, subtlety; also sickness, pain, loss, disaster, snares of enemies; self-confidence, will; the Querent if male.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Physician, Magus, mental disease, disgrace, disquiet.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Will, Will-Power, Dexterity.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Will applied to evil ends, Weakness of Will, Cunning, Knavishness.

### II. THE HIGH PRIESTESS (Female Pope / Popess)
**Hebrew Letter:** Gimel (ג) — Camel; Moon; Uniting Intelligence; joins Kether to Tiphareth
**Astronomy:** Moon
**Foster Case:** Gimel = Moon; "Peace and Strife: implied by commerce; symbolized by the bow of Artemis"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A woman crowned with high mitre or tiara, head encircled by veil, stole/solar cross upon breast, Book of Science open in hand. Represents **Science, Wisdom, Knowledge.**
- **Waite:** Seated between pillars B and J (Boaz and Jachin). Crown of Isis (crescent horns + full moon disk). Blue robe flowing. Cross on breast. Scroll of Torah partly concealed by her mantle. Crescent moon at feet. She is the esoteric, withdrawn power — keeper of hidden wisdom.
- **Ouspensky:** "Occultism. Esoterism. Mysticism. Theosophy. Initiation. Isis. Mystery."
- **Papus (Card 2):** The Passive Absolute. Corresponds to He (1st). God the Son, Isis, Vishnu, Juno. Preserving feminine force. The Astral.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Secrets, mystery, the future as yet unrevealed; the woman who interests the Querent (if male); the Querent herself (if female); silence, tenacity; mystery, wisdom, science.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Passion, moral or physical ardour, conceit, surface knowledge.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Science, Wisdom, Knowledge, Education.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Conceit, Ignorance, Unskilfulness, Superficial Knowledge.

### III. THE EMPRESS
**Hebrew Letter:** Daleth (ד) — Door/Womb; Venus; Illuminating Intelligence; joins Chokmah to Binah
**Astronomy:** Venus
**Foster Case:** Daleth = Venus; "Knowledge and Ignorance: as Buddhi, principle of judgment"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A winged and crowned woman seated on a throne, sceptre bearing a globe surmounted by a cross in one hand, other resting on shield with eagle. Symbol of **Action, the result of the union of Science and Will.**
- **Waite:** A beautiful woman seated in lush field of grain, shield with eagle, sceptre topped with globe, crown of twelve stars. She is Nature fecund, the Great Mother. Venus's symbol is on her heart-shaped shield.
- **Ouspensky:** "Nature in its phenomenal aspect. The ever renewing and re-creating force of Nature. The objective reality."
- **Papus (Card 3):** The Transformer/Physical. Vau of Tetragrammaton. God the Holy Ghost, Horus, Siva, Vulcan. The mercurial equilibrating force.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Fruitfulness, action, initiative, length of days; the unknown, clandestine; also difficulty, doubt, ignorance.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Light, truth, the unravelling of involved matters, public rejoicings; vacillation.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Action, Plan, Undertaking, Movement in a matter, Initiative.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Inaction, Frittering away of power, Want of Concentration, Vacillation.

### IV. THE EMPEROR
**Hebrew Letter:** Heh (ה) — Window; Aries; Constituting Intelligence; joins Chokmah to Tiphareth
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Aries
**Foster Case:** Heh = Aries; "Sight: vision, contemplation, discernment"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** He is crowned, leaning against throne, legs form a cross, shield with eagle beside him. Right hand bears sceptre. Body and arms form triangle above cross. Represents **Realisation.**
- **Waite:** Crowned monarch, commanding, seated on throne with rams'-head arms (Aries symbol). Holds ankh-sceptre and globe. He is virile power, realization, the power of this world. "The higher kingship, occupying the intellectual throne."
- **Ouspensky:** "Tetragrammaton. The law of four. Latent energy of Nature. Logos in the full aspect with all possibilities of the new Logos. Hermetic philosophy."
- **Papus (Card 4):** The Generator. 2nd He. A second Yod in germ. Synthesis of the first three letters.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Stability, power, protection, realization; a great person; aid, reason, conviction; authority and will.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Benevolence, compassion, credit; confusion to enemies, obstruction, immaturity.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Realisation, Effect, Development.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Stoppage, Check, Immature, Unripe.

### V. THE HIEROPHANT (Pope / Papal Figure)
**Hebrew Letter:** Vau (ו) — Nail; Taurus; Triumphant and Eternal Intelligence; joins Chokmah to Chesed
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Taurus
**Foster Case:** Vau = Taurus; "Hearing: unites man to man by speech, and man to Spirit by the Word"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** Crowned with papal tiara, seated between two pillars of Hermes and Solomon. Right hand makes sign of esoterism; left holds staff with triple cross. Two ministers kneel before him. Symbol of **Mercy and Beneficence.**
- **Waite:** Triple crown, seated between two pillars (not those of the High Priestess). Sceptre with triple cross. Crossed keys at feet. Two priests in albs kneel before him. "He is the ruling power of external religion, as the High Priestess is the prevailing genius of the esoteric, withdrawn power." He is exoteric orthodox doctrine.
- **Ouspensky (as Card V — Chariot in his numbering):** "Man. The Imagination. Magic. Self-suggestion. Self deceit. Artificial means of attainment. Pseudo-occultism."
- **Papus (Card 5):** He = 1st He; the passive force balanced by the active. Symbol of the preserved feminine.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Marriage, alliance, captivity, servitude; mercy and goodness; inspiration; the man to whom the Querent has recourse.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Society, good understanding, concord, overkindness, weakness.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Mercy, Beneficence, Kindness, Goodness.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Over-kindness, Weakness, Foolish exercise of generosity.

### VI. THE LOVERS
**Hebrew Letter:** Zain (ז) — Sword; Gemini; Disposing Intelligence; joins Binah to Tiphareth
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Gemini
**Foster Case:** Zain = Gemini; "Smell: keen perception, sagacity, discrimination"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** Usually described as Man between Vice and Virtue while a winged genius threatens Vice. Mathers: "I am rather inclined to the opinion that it represents the Qabalistical Microprosopus between Binah and Malkuth." Signifies **Wise Disposition / Proof or Trial.**
- **Waite:** The sun shines in the zenith; great winged figure (Raphael/angel) with arms extended pours down influences. Two human figures, male and female, unveiled. Behind the man: Tree of Life with twelve fruits; behind the woman: Tree of Knowledge with serpent. "This is in all simplicity the card of human love, here exhibited as part of the way, the truth and the life." A mystery of the Covenant and Sabbath.
- **Ouspensky:** "Man. Another aspect of the Adam Kadmon, the Perfect Man, The divine androgyne. Love as the efforts of Adam Kadmon to find himself. The equilibrium of contraries. The unification of the duad, as the means of attaining the Light."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Attraction, love, beauty, trials overcome.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Failure, foolish designs; marriage frustrated and contrarieties of all kinds.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Wise Dispositions, Proof, Trials Surmounted.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Unwise Plans, Failure when put to the test.

### VII. THE CHARIOT
**Hebrew Letter:** Cheth (ח) — Field; Cancer; Intelligence of Influence; joins Binah to Geburah
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Cancer
**Foster Case:** Cheth = Cancer; "Speech: mastery of language is mastery of thought"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A Conqueror crowned and bearing sceptre, riding in a cubical chariot, surmounted by four columns and canopy, drawn by two horses (one straight ahead, one turned). Represents **Triumph, Victory of Justice and Judgment.**
- **Waite:** An erect and princely figure carrying a drawn sword. On his shoulders: Urim and Thummim. Two sphinxes (one black, one white) draw the chariot. "He is above all things triumph in the mind." He has replied to the sphinx. His conquests are manifest/external — he cannot open the scroll of Tora.
- **Ouspensky (as Card VII Hierophant):** "Mysticism. Theosophy. Esoteric side of all religions."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Succour, providence; also war, triumph, presumption, vengeance, trouble.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Riot, quarrel, dispute, litigation, defeat.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Triumph, Victory, Overcoming obstacles.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Overthrown, Conquered by Obstacles at the last moment.

### VIII. STRENGTH (Fortitude)
**Hebrew Letter:** Teth (ט) — Serpent; Leo; Intelligence of the Secret; joins Chesed to Geburah
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Leo
**Foster Case:** Teth = Leo; "Taste: refinement; experience; enduring patience, fortitude"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A woman crowned, who calmly and without effort closes the jaws of a furious lion. Represents **Strength.**
- **Waite:** A woman (lemniscate ∞ above head, same as Magician) closing a lion's jaws — the lion is already subdued, led by a chain of flowers. "Her beneficent fortitude has already subdued the lion." The chain of flowers = the sweet yoke and light burden of Divine Law. "The card has nothing to do with self-confidence in the ordinary sense... it concerns the confidence of those whose strength is God."
- **Ouspensky:** "The Real Power. Strength of love. Strength of Union (Magic chain). Strength of the Infinite. Occultism. Esoterism. Theosophy."
- **Note:** Waite transposed VIII (Strength) and XI (Justice) — traditional numbering has Justice=8, Strength=11.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Power, energy, action, courage, magnanimity; complete success and honours.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Despotism, abuse of power, weakness, discord, sometimes disgrace.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Power, Might, Force, Strength, Fortitude.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Abuse of Power, Overbearingness, Want of Fortitude.

### IX. THE HERMIT
**Hebrew Letter:** Yod (י) — Hand; Virgo; Intelligence of Will; joins Chesed to Tiphareth
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Virgo
**Foster Case:** Yod = Virgo; "Hand: dexterity, skill; power, might, supremacy; symbol of the Supreme Spirit"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** An old and bearded man wrapped in a mantle, head covered with cowl, bearing the lantern of occult science in right hand, magic wand half-hidden in left. He is **Prudence.**
- **Waite:** The Hermit holds up his beacon on an eminence — a star shines in the lantern. "Where I am, you also may be." He blends the idea of the Ancient of Days with the Light of the World. Not the occult hermit of personal magnetism — rather, the truth that Divine Mysteries secure their own protection from those unprepared.
- **Ouspensky:** "Man. The Path to the Initiation. Seeking for truth in the right way. Inner Knowledge. Inner Light. Inner Force. Theosophy. Occultism."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Prudence, circumspection; also treason, dissimulation, roguery, corruption.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Concealment, disguise, policy, fear, unreasoned caution.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Prudence, Caution, Deliberation.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Over-prudence, Timorousness, Fear.

### X. WHEEL OF FORTUNE
**Hebrew Letter:** Kaph (כ) — Grasping Hand; Jupiter; Intelligence of Conciliation; joins Chesed to Netzach
**Astronomy:** Jupiter
**Foster Case:** Kaph = Jupiter; "Wealth and Poverty: the extremes of 'property'"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A wheel of seven spokes revolving between two uprights. On ascending side: animal ascending; on descending: monkey descending; both forms bound to wheel. Above: angel (or sphinx) holding sword and crown. Symbolizes **Fortune, good or bad.**
- **Waite:** Follows Lévi's reconstruction. Typhon (serpent) on descending side; Anubis ascending; sphinx balanced above. TARO/ROTA inscribed with the letters of the Divine Name. "The transliteration of Taro as Rota is inscribed on the wheel, counterchanged with the letters of the Divine Name — to shew that Providence is implied through all." Four Living Creatures of Ezekiel at the corners. "The perpetual motion of a fluidic universe and the flux of human life."
- **Ouspensky:** "The Wheel of Life. The life ever changing and ever remaining the same. The Circle of Time and the four elements. The idea of the circle."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Destiny, fortune, success, elevation, luck, felicity.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Increase, abundance, superfluity.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Good Fortune, Success, Unexpected Luck.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Ill-Fortune, Failure, Unexpected Ill-Luck.

### XI. JUSTICE
**Hebrew Letter:** Lamed (ל) — Ox-goad; Libra; Faithful Intelligence; joins Geburah to Tiphareth
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Libra
**Foster Case:** Lamed = Libra; "Work: action; Karma"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A woman crowned and seated on a throne (between two columns), right hand holding upright sword, left hand holding scales. Symbolizes **Equilibrium and Justice.**
- **Waite:** "The moral principle which deals unto every man according to his works... differs in its essence from the spiritual justice which is involved in the idea of election." Seated between pillars, like the High Priestess. "The pillars of Justice open into one world and the pillars of the High Priestess into another."
- **Ouspensky:** "Truth. Real Knowledge. Inner Truth. Occultism. Esoterism. Theosophy."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Equity, rightness, probity, executive; triumph of the deserving side in law.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Law in all its departments, legal complications, bigotry, bias, excessive severity.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Equilibrium, Balance, Justice.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Bigotry, Want of Balance, Abuse of Justice, Over-severity, Inequality, Bias.

### XII. THE HANGED MAN
**Hebrew Letter:** Mem (מ) — Water; Stable Intelligence; joins Geburah to Hod
**Element:** Water (Mem is one of the 3 Mother Letters)
**Foster Case:** Mem = Water; "the first mirror; reproduction; reflected life"

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A man hung head downward from a gibbet by one foot, hands bound behind back so body forms triangle with point down; legs form cross above. Two sacks/weights at armpits. Symbolizes **Sacrifice.**
- **Waite:** Gallows forms a Tau cross; the suspended figure — from leg position — forms a fylfot cross. Nimbus about the head. Living wood with leaves. "The face expresses deep entrancement, not suffering; the figure suggests life in suspension, but life and not death." One of the most profound cards. "We may exhaust all published interpretations and find only vanity."
- **Ouspensky:** "Man. The Pain of the higher consciousness bound by the limitations of the body and mind. Superman in the separate man."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Wisdom, circumspection, discernment, trials, sacrifice, intuition, divination, prophecy.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Selfishness, the crowd, body politic.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Self-sacrifice, Sacrifice, Devotion, Bound.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Selfishness, Unbound, Partial sacrifice.

### XIII. DEATH
**Hebrew Letter:** Nun (נ) — Fish; Scorpio; Imaginative Intelligence; joins Tiphareth to Netzach
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Scorpio
**Foster Case:** Nun = Scorpio; Nun meaning = Fish (like Joshua son of Nun, like Jesus Ichthys)

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A skeleton armed with a scythe, mowing down heads in a meadow like grass. Signifies **Transformation, or Change.**
- **Waite:** A skeleton in black armour rides a white horse. Before him fall a king, a child, a maiden; a bishop prays. In the distance: two pillars, the sun rising between them. The black flag with a white rose. "The pale horse, and his name that sat on him was Death." Yet the sun rises — it is transformation, not cessation.
- **Ouspensky:** "Another aspect of Life. Going away in order to come back at the same time. Completion of the circle."
- **Papus (Card 13):** Hebrew letter Mem. The transformation of the divine world, regeneration under the influence of death.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** End, mortality, destruction, corruption; for a man, the loss of a benefactor; for a woman, many contrarieties; for a maid, failure of marriage projects.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Inertia, sleep, lethargy, petrifaction, somnambulism; hope destroyed.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Death, Change, Transformation, Alteration for the worse.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Death just escaped, Partial change, Alteration for the better.

### XIV. TEMPERANCE
**Hebrew Letter:** Samekh (ס) — Prop; Sagittarius; Tentative Intelligence; joins Tiphareth to Yesod
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Sagittarius

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** An angel with sign of the Sun on her brow pouring liquid from one vessel into another. Represents **Combination.**
- **Waite:** A great winged angel (one foot on land, one in water) pours liquid between two cups in an endless flow. Triangle within square on chest. Irises grow at the water's edge. A path leads to a distant mountain with a crown of light. The figure is on the path between the material world and the spiritual.
- **Ouspensky:** "The first attainment. The Arcanum Magnum of the occultists. The Fourth Dimension. Higher space. Eternal Now."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Economy, moderation, frugality, management, accommodation.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Things connected with churches, religions, sects, the priesthood; also disunion, unfortunate combinations, competing interests.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Combination, Conformation, Uniting.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Ill-advised combinations, Disunion, Clashing interests.

### XV. THE DEVIL
**Hebrew Letter:** Ayin (ע) — Eye; Capricorn; Renovating Intelligence; joins Tiphareth to Hod
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Capricorn

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A horned and winged demon with eagle's claws, standing on an altar to which two smaller devils are bound by a collar and cord. Flame-headed sceptre in left hand. Image of **Fate or Fatality, good or evil.**
- **Waite:** Baphomet-like figure on a half-cube pedestal. Inverted pentagram above. Two human figures (echoing the Lovers) are chained to the pedestal, but loosely — they could free themselves. "The figure has no wings; but the Horns are those of the Sacred Goat of the Sabbath, the Star between the Horns is the Blazing Pentagram reversed." The card shows bondage to matter and illusion.
- **Ouspensky:** "Man. Weakness. Falsehood. The Fall of man into separateness, into hatred and into finiteness."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Ravage, violence, vehemence, extraordinary efforts, force, fatality; that which is predestined but is not for this reason evil.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Evil fatality, weakness, pettiness, blindness.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Fatality for Good.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Fatality for Evil.

---

### XVI. THE TOWER (Lightning-Struck Tower / House of God)
**Hebrew Letter:** Peh (פ) — Mouth; Mars; Exciting Intelligence; joins Netzach to Hod
**Astronomy:** Mars

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A Tower whose upper part is like a crown, struck by a lightning-flash. Two men fall headlong (one forming a Hebrew letter Ayin). Sparks and debris falling. Shows **Ruin, Disruption.**
- **Waite:** A Tower struck by lightning from a dark cloud, flame bursting from windows, crown flying off, two figures falling. "The card foretells catastrophe, whether the ruin of plans or the destruction of the Querent's world by the power which he himself has built in opposition to the natural order."
- **Ouspensky:** "Sectarianism. Tower of Babel. Exoterism. Confusion of tongues. Fall of exoterism. The force of Nature re-establishing the truth distorted by men."
- **Papus (Card 16 / Hebrew Zain again):** "The lightning-struck Tower" — disruption of the material world, the overthrow of false structures.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Misery, distress, indigence, adversity, calamity, disgrace, deception, ruin. A card in particular of unforeseen catastrophe.
- **Waite (Reversed):** The same in lesser degree; also oppression, imprisonment, tyranny.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Ruin, Disruption, Over-throw, Loss, Bankruptcy.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** These in a more or less partial degree.

### XVII. THE STAR
**Hebrew Letter:** Tzaddi (צ) — Fish-hook; Aquarius; Natural Intelligence; joins Netzach to Yesod
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Aquarius

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** A nude female figure pours water upon the earth from two vases. In the heavens above: the Blazing Star of the Magi surrounded by seven others. Trees and plants grow beneath her magic influence; butterfly of Psyche alights on one. She is the **Star of Hope.**
- **Waite:** A nude woman kneels at a pool, pouring water from two cups — one into the pool, one onto the land. The great eight-pointed star (Sirius?) shines overhead, with seven smaller stars. An ibis perches in a tree. The figure has one foot on land, one in water — balance of conscious and unconscious. She is eternal youth and beauty.
- **Ouspensky:** "The real aspect of the Astral World. That which may be seen in ecstasy. The imagination of Nature. Real Knowledge. Occultism."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Loss, theft, privation, abandonment; also hope and bright prospects.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Arrogance, haughtiness, impotence.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Hope, Expectation, Bright promises.
- **Mathers (Reversed):** Hopes not fulfilled, Expectations disappointed or fulfilled in a minor degree.

---

### XVIII. THE MOON
**Hebrew Letter:** Qoph (ק) — Back of Head; Pisces; Corporeal Intelligence; joins Netzach to Malkuth
**Astronomy/Zodiac:** Pisces

**Symbolism:**
- **Mathers:** The moon shining in the heavens, drops of dew falling, wolf and dog howling at the moon, halted at the foot of two towers. A path loses itself in the horizon sprinkled with drops of blood. A crayfish (Cancer/Scorpio symbol) crawls through water toward land. Symbolizes **Twilight, Deception, and Error.**
- **Waite:** The full moon with face, between two towers. A lobster/crayfish emerging from a pool. A dog and wolf howl at the moon. A path leads between the towers into the distance. "Reflections of every kind — the light of the moon, the howling of beasts, the crawling of crawling things."
- **Ouspensky:** "The Astral World as it is seen by the artificial means of magic. Psychic, spiritistic world. Dreads of the night. The real light from above and the false representation of that light from below. Pseudo-mysticism."

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Waite (Upright):** Hidden enemies, danger, calumny, darkness, terror, deception, occult forces, error.
- **Waite (Reversed):** Instability, inconstancy, silence, lesser degrees of deception and error.
- **Mathers (Upright):** Twilight, Deception, Error.

## PART 3: SPREADS & DIVINATION METHODS

### Method 1: THE ANCIENT CELTIC METHOD (Waite — "Celtic Cross")
*The most famous spread in Western tarot, first published by Waite in 1911.*

**Preparation:** Select a Significator card (represents the person or matter in question):
- Knight = man over 40; King = man under 40; Queen = woman over 40; Page = girl under 40
- Wands = very fair (yellow/auburn hair, blue eyes); Cups = light brown/dull fair hair, grey/blue eyes; Swords = hazel/grey eyes, dark brown hair; Pentacles = very dark hair, dark eyes, sallow complexion.
- Note: temperament overrides appearance — a very dark energetic person → Swords; very fair lethargic person → Cups.

**Card Positions:**
1. **This covers him** — The general influence affecting the person/matter; the atmosphere.
2. **This crosses him** — The nature of the obstacles. (Laid across Card 1)
3. **This crowns him** — The Querent's aim or ideal; the best achievable but not yet actual. (Above)
4. **This is beneath him** — The foundation/basis; what has already passed into actuality. (Below)
5. **This is behind him** — The influence just passed or passing away. (Behind, where Significator looks from)
6. **This is before him** — The influence coming into action in the near future. (Ahead, where Significator faces)

*Cards 1–6 form a cross. Cards 7–10 are placed in a vertical column to the right:*

7. **Himself** — The Significator's position or attitude in the circumstances.
8. **His house** — His environment, tendencies at work, position in life, influence of immediate friends.
9. **His hopes or fears** — What the Querent hopes for or fears.
10. **What will come** — The final result, the culmination brought about by all the other influences.

*If the 10th card is dubious, take it as the new Significator and repeat the spread for more detail.*
*If the 10th card is a Court Card, the matter falls ultimately into the hands of that person.*

### Method 2: MATHERS' FIRST METHOD (Horseshoe)
Shuffle and cut 78 cards three times. Deal into two piles (A and B) with pattern 2-1-2-1: deal to B, B, A, B, B, A...
- Pile A = 26 cards; Pile B = 52 cards
- Subdivide B into C (17 cards) and D (35 cards) using same method
- Subdivide D into E (11 cards) and F (24 cards); F is set aside
- Read A, C, E in horseshoe shape from right to left, combining pairs (1st+26th, 2nd+25th, etc.)

### Method 3: MATHERS' SECOND METHOD (Every 7th Card)
- Withdraw Significator. Shuffle and cut three times.
- Draw every 7th card to get 21 cards.
- Arrange in 3 rows of 7, reading right to left. Combine paired positions.

### Method 4: PAPUS — ETTEILLA'S METHOD (Four Deals)
*From Papus, Lesson 6, based on Etteilla's original unpublished method:*
- Four successive deals, each refining the reading
- 1st Deal: Overall situation
- 2nd Deal: Past influences
- 3rd Deal: Present circumstances
- 4th Deal: Future outcome

### Method 5: WAITE'S ALTERNATIVE METHOD (Julia Orsini / French Method)
- Shuffle entire pack; some cards inverted.
- Querent cuts with left hand.
- Deal first 42 cards in 6 packets of 7 face up.
- Take up each packet and re-deal into 7 new packets of 6.
- Take top card of each → 7 cards (1st line); next 2 from each → 14 cards (2 lines); remaining 21 cards → 3 lines.
- 6 horizontal lines of 7 cards total.
- Male Querent = Magician; Female = High Priestess.

### Method 6: GRAND ORIENT — The Golden Wheel of Fortune
*Historical system attributed to Cagliostro, taken from a Latin manuscript on Astrology:*
- Partly numerical, partly astrological, and wholly magical.
- A circular/wheel arrangement of cards.
- Said to have been used by Cagliostro in his divinatory practice.

**Eliphas Lévi (quoted by Foster Case):**

> "This Egyptian book, the sole remains of their superb libraries, exists to our day; it is even so common that no savant has designed to trouble himself about it, no one before myself having suspected its illustrious origin. This book is the PACK OF TAROT CARDS."

**Foster Case (Preface, 1919):**
> "Its rich symbolism and ingenious construction make the Tarot the best of all instruments for true occult education, i.e., for 'drawing out' the wisdom hidden in the heart of man."
> "As infinity in numbers loses itself in the unit which is their basis, and as the innumerable rays of a circle are united in a single center, so it is also with the Mysteries; their hieroglyphics and infinitude of emblems have the object of exemplifying but one single truth."

**Waite (Pictorial Key, Preface):**
> "As poetry is the most beautiful expression of the things that are of all most beautiful, so is symbolism the most catholic expression in concealment of things that are most profound in the Sanctuary."

**Ouspensky (Symbolism of the Tarot):**
> "No study of occult philosophy is possible without an acquaintance with symbolism, for if the words occultism and symbolism are correctly used, they mean almost one and the same thing."
> "The Tarot represents Nature in all the richness of its infinite possibilities, and there is in it as in Nature, not one but all potential meanings. And these meanings are fluent and ever-changing, so the Tarot cannot be specifically this or that, for it ever moves and yet is ever the same."
> "The soul itself is a point having no dimension in the world of the spirit, symbolized by the triangle. It is clear that such an idea could not have originated with ignorant people and clear also that the Tarot is something more than a pack of playing or fortune-telling cards."

**Grand Orient (Preface, 1909):**
> "Whatever the process, whatever the instruments, they are simply aids to elicit clairvoyance, and to cast the Seer for the time being into a subjective or interior condition."

**Papus (Tarot of the Bohemians, Preface):**
> "The Tarot pack of cards, transmitted by the Gypsies from generation to generation, is the primitive book of ancient initiation."

**Frater Achad (Ever-Coming Son, Preface):**
> "The Tarot Trumps, being a sort of Universal Alphabet, may of course be interpreted in a variety of ways... If by this means we obtain 'More Light on the Tarot Trumps' and the Universal Tradition, which transcends what we call Light and Darkness, becomes plainer to my readers, the book will not have been written in vain."

**Mathers (The Tarot, on TARO as word):**

## PART 6: UNIQUE INSIGHTS BY AUTHOR

### Paul Foster Case
- Emphasizes that nearly all published Tarot attributions were based on Lévi's **deliberately incorrect** Hebrew letter assignments.
- His own attribution (published 1919) was independently derived, starting from Court de Gébelin's position of the zero card and Lévi's statement that cards illustrate occult meaning of the Hebrew alphabet.
- Key insight: The Tetragrammaton (Yod-He-Vau-He) is the master key to understanding both Major and Minor Arcana structure.
- The four suits = four worlds of the Qabalah: Atziluth (Wands), Beriah (Cups), Yetzirah (Swords), Assiah (Pentacles).

### A.E. Waite
- Insists the Tarot has been "rescued" from cartomancers and occultists who have brought it into contempt.
- The Rider-Waite deck (illustrated by Pamela Colman Smith) introduced illustrated pip cards for the first time.
- Celtic Cross spread published here for the first time in connection with Tarot.
- Waite transposed Justice (VIII→XI) and Strength (XI→VIII) for doctrinal reasons.
- The Fool is placed at the beginning (0) — before the Magician.

### Papus (Gérard Encausse)
- Most systematic structural analysis: three Septenaries + Ternary of Transition.
- Every card has three levels of meaning: Divine/Superlative, Human, Material.
- Provides the most detailed divination lessons (7 lessons in Chapter XX).
- Connects each card to specific astronomical, botanical, mineral, and anatomical correspondences.
- Affinities table shows which Major Arcana "governs" which Minor Arcana cards.

### P.D. Ouspensky
- Most philosophically ambitious interpretation — connects Tarot to Nietzsche, Plotinus, and modern psychology.
- Cards taken in pairs (I and 0; II and XXI; III and XX etc.) — each pair makes a complete philosophical whole.
- "A symbol may serve to transfer our intuitions and to suggest new ones only so long as its meaning is not defined. Real symbols are perpetually in process of creation."
- Triangle (21 Major cards) + Point (Fool = 0) + Square (56 Minor cards) = symbolic representation of God, Man, Universe.

### S.L. MacGregor Mathers
- Earliest systematic English-language tarot guide (1888).
- Provides the famous mnemonic sentence reading all 22 Majors in sequence.
- Distinguishes Marseilles Italian pack as best for divination.
- Court cards represent persons: Swords = very dark; Pentacles = not so dark; Cups = rather fair; Wands = much fairer.
- Multiple cards together: many Wands = feasting; many Cups = lovemaking; Swords = quarrelling; Coins = money.

### Frater Achad
- Proposes "Reformed Order of the Paths" on the Tree of Life — reversing the traditional direction of the Serpent's ascent.
- Connects Tarot to Egyptian solar theology, specifically the Aten worship of Akhenaten.
- New Aeon / Cycle of Aquarius themes — the Tarot as living document for the current age.
- Demonstrates that astrological symbolism becomes more harmonious under the Reformed arrangement.

## PART 8: RECURRENCE OF CARDS IN DEALING (Waite)

When the same card or type of card appears multiple times in a reading:

**Four Aces:** Great power and force.
**Four Kings:** Great honour and success.
**Four Queens:** Authority, influence.
**Four Knights:** Meetings with important persons; if reversed, disquiet.
**Four Pages:** Dangerous illness; if reversed, accidents with young persons.
**Four Tens:** Condemned to a heavy sentence.
**Four Nines:** A good friend; if reversed, rich and libertine enemy.
**Four Eights:** Great shock; if reversed, fight and dispute.
**Four Sevens:** Conspiracy of weak persons against the Querent; if reversed, good counsel.
**Four Sixes:** Abundance.
